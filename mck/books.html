Book Review of the Week

This would be the first book I would recommend anyone trying to more with themselves. The reason I would recommend this one first, because this book highlights how everyone is different and needs to operate in different ways. Most may be aware that they themselves can’t operate like their peers, but they do it anyways and that in itself is a problem. Operating in a way that is suboptimal can be detrimental to reaching one’s own potential. The journey of self-growth can not start until you take charge of you own life and I believe that starts with being able to properly manage yourself.
